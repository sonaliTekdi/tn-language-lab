How To Learn For Tomorrow
Across the world, digital tools which allow learners to speak, record, and listen to themselves have been recognised as having a significant role in improving listening and communication among non-native speakers learning foreign languages. It plays an important role in learner’s acquiring accurate pronunciation through stress on word accent, intonation, and rhythm.
The online learning platform would enable the upper primary learners in the socially and educationally backward blocks to practice multiplicity of linguistic activities such as listening exercises, vocabulary work, and fluency building. The platform also integrates AI in language learning by enabling students to record and practice their speech which would then be tested for fluency and accuracy by AI.
Mozhigal proposes to supplement existing Hi-Tech Lab computers in schools with two pairs of headsets per system to enable their functioning as language labs.
Once every 2 weeks, 1 English period will be dedicated for student use of the language lab. This will result in each child spending a combined 20 periods a year in the language lab for learning English.
The language lab period would be utilized as a self paced independent mode of learning where students will be able to access level-based modules on the computer and engage in listening as well as speaking activities.

Break the Barriers
“Mozhigal” builds a conducive environment for the student to be the self paced independent language learners. Language breaks the barriers of communication, shapes the way one thinks and opens up diversified opportunities to leverage.
The Students and teachers will use Mozhigal’s interactive and engaging mechanics to learn and practice listening, speaking, reading and writing.
