Approach and Design for Language Lab Portal
The School Education Department conducted research on the existing frameworks for learning foreign languages like English. This was combined with study of good practices in child-friendly interfaces to learn any language. To actualise a thorough foundation embedded into digital pedagogy and AI powered feedback, the department applied these design principles for “Mozhigal”:
Post Method Pedagogy :
Post method pedagogy enables teachers to get location-specific and classroom-oriented innovative practices. It guides the teachers to take theoretical teaching material and make it adaptive to classroom reality [ Kumaravadivelu, 1994]. The method focuses on giving teachers the freedom to adapt classroom material according to their students’ learning levels and regional context.
Constructivism :
Constructivism sees learning as an active process where learning is built through experience. Problem solving and understanding skills are grown through real tasks, experiential learning and collaboration. Children will be encouraged to read stories and correct themselves by the AI powered mechanics. They will create stories by selecting pictures and recording audio. This method promotes learners’ motivation and critical thinking as well as encourages them to learn independently [Amineh & Davatgari, 2015].
Bilingual Approach :
The bilingual approach makes the unfamiliar foreign language learning easier by using the native tongue as the base language in the teaching-learning material delivery. In Mozhigal, to initiate children into English learning, the medium of instruction in Level 1 will be bilingual i.e available in English as well as Tamil. The child will build the skill to independently understand and follow instructions in the modules. It will also make it easier for the teacher to facilitate the sessions in English and Tamil. Cummins’s Common Underlying Proficiency (CUP) theory assumes that students will be able to draw on their first language to be able to function at an acceptable standard in the second language. [Cummins, 2000].
Multimodal learning :
This in simple terms is the use of multimedia as TLM for students - auditory, visual , kinesthetic and reading/writing materials [Birch and Sankey, 2008]. The School Education Department believes that by adding engaging interactive activities such as a partner based read along with feedback, a speech practice tool and a story creation tool, all senses of the child will be thoroughly engaged.
Peer learning :
Promoting collaboration by peers coordinating and learning with one another. The Read Along tool has a feature that allows two students to exchange dialogue and play a “2 player reading game”. Additionally, our “Let’s Talk” Sections in the lesson modules also promote peer learning and collaboration.
